|ˈdiviˌdend|

DEFINITION: an amount of money distributed by a business to its shareholders. The amount and regularity of dividend payments is determined by the board of directors.

Dividends are measured in both dollar amounts and as a percentage of the price of the stock.

Dividends are issued per share, and may be in the form of cash, stock or property. Older, well-established companies typically offer the highest dividends.

Emerging and growing companies usually offer small dividends, if any, in order to reinvest their earnings to ensure future profit.
