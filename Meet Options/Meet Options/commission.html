|kəˈmiSHən|

DEFINITION: a fee or service charge that clients pay to their broker. Brokers charge commissions for placing trades, providing advice, and other services.

Each broker or brokerage has a different commission schedule. Commissions provide the primary source of income for brokers.

Most brokers charge a flat commission for stock orders, regardless of the number of shares purchased.

Conversely, commissions for option trades usually change depending on how many contracts are ordered. The more contracts ordered, the higher the commission will be usually.

Commission fees are one of the most important discriminators to evaluate when selecting a broker.
