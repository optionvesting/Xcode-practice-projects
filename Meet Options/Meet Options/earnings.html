DEFINITION: the amount of profit made by a company over a given period of time (usually measured every three calendar months, or quarter).

Earnings are one of the most important factors affecting the price of a stock. Projected and predicted future earnings also provide pressure to the stock market to typically refer to after-tax net income.

Economic analysts are constantly making projections regarding the earnings of a company. When the actual earnings amounts varies greatly from the analysts' expectations, the market usually reacts very quickly by correcting the stock price based on the knewly known earnings.
