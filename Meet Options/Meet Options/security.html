|siˈkyo͝oritē|

DEFINITION: a financial instrument that has financial value, and is traded at an exchange. The company that issues the security is called the issuer. There are several different types of securities:

A stock represents ownership in a publicly-traded corporation. Stocks are issued to secure capital while giving up part ownership in the company.

A bond represents a creditor relationship with governmental body or a corporation. Bonds are issued to secure capital as a loan without exchanging part ownership in the company.

An option represents the rights to buy or sell stock.

There are two main types of securities: debt and equity. Debt securities include bonds, CDs, and preferred stock. Common stock is a type of equity.

In the United States, the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and other self-regulatory organizations (such as the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority) regulate the public offer and sale of securities.
