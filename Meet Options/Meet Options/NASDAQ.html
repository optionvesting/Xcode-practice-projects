DEFINITION: the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations is a virtual trading exchange, that facilitates trading and provides quotes on more than 5,000 stocks. Created in 1971, the Nasdaq was the world's first electronic stock market.

Stocks traded on the Nasdaq usually have four or five letter ticker symbols, as opposed to stocks on the NYSE, which usually have three or less symbols.

The term "Nasdaq" used to be capitalized "NASDAQ" as an acronym for National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotation. The acronym is no longer used and Nasdaq is now a proper noun.

The Nasdaq is traditionally home to many high-tech stocks, such as Apple, Google, Intel, Dell and Cisco.
