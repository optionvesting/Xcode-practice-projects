DEFINITION: an index that tracks 500 stocks, each of which is chosen based on market cap and other factors. The S&P 500 is designed to be a leading benchmark of U.S. stock performance.

The S&P Index Committee, a team of analysts and economists at Standard & Poor's, selects the 500 stocks in the index. The S&P 500 is a market value weighted index - each stock's weight is proportionate to its market value.

Standard & Poor's also has an index of small cap stocks called the S&P 600, and an index of mid cap stocks called the S&P 400.

There are exchange traded funds (ETFs) and index funds that allow individual traders to achieve the same returns as the S&P 500. Options are also available for trading on some of these funds (i.e. ticker symbol SPY).
