DEFINITION: the Dow Jones Industrial Average is a price-weighted average of 30 significant stocks traded on the New York Stock Exchange and the NASDAQ. The average bares the name of its inventor, Charles Dow, and was created at the end of the 19th century.

Often referred to as "the Dow," the DJIA is the most important stock index in the world. It provides a reflection of overall market performance.

The DJIA includes companies like General Electric, Disney, Exxon and Microsoft.
