|ˌväləˈtilitē|

DEFINITION: a measure of how much the price of a security changes in relation to the rest of the market. The higher the volatility, the more risk and more reward provided by the security.

Beta is the primary measure of volatility. A beta value of 1.0 is the baseline value. A stock with a beta > 1 is more volatile, and a beta of < 1 is less volatile.
