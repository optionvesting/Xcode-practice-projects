DEFINITION: the total dollar value of all the shares of a particular stock available on the market. Market capitalization is calculated by multiplying the number of available shares of stock by the current market price of one share.

The market cap is used as a measure of how large a business is. In recent years, the title of the most valuable business has gone back and forth between Apple and ExxonMobil.

Companies and their stock are broken up into three main categories based on their market capitalization: Large Cap: > $10 billion

Mid Cap: $2 billion to $10 billion

Small Cap: < $2 billion
