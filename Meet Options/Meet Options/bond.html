DEFINITION: an investment in debt, comparable to providing a loan to a business or governmental entity. When you purchase bonds, you are purchasing debt and will receive a return on your purchase, similar to interest.

Commercial and governmental entities sell bonds to generate cash and fund projects.

Bonds are a fixed-income security, meaning they have a set rate of return for the purchaser, unless the entity defaults on the debt, in which case the purchaser of the bond loses their money.

The interest rate paid to the purchaser of the bond is called a "coupon," and is paid in full on the bond's maturity date. Most bonds are issued in six months increments.
