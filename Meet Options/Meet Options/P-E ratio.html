|pē| |ē|ˈ|rāSHē ō|

DEFINITION: a ratio of a company's current share price compared to its per-share earnings. To calculate P/E, divide a stock's price by it's Earnings Per Share (EPS).

For example, if a stock is currently trading at $100 a share and its earnings over the last 12 months were $10 per share, the P/E would be 10 ($100/$10).

To calculate trailing P/E, use the EPS over the last four quarters. To calculate a projected P/E, use the expected EPS over the next 4 quarters.

P/E is sometimes called a "price multiple" or "earnings multiple".

Investors often compare the P/E of one stock to other stocks in the same sector. A high P/E relative to other stocks in the sector could indicate the stock is expected to have more growth than other stocks, or it is overpriced.

P/E should never be used as a sole determining factor when deciding what stocks to buy or sell.
