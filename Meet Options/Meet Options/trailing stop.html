DEFINITION: a stop-loss order that rises with the price of the stock or option. This feature allows you to lock in gains and limit risk, and is a very useful type of order.

For example, you buy a stock for $50. You don't want to lose more than 10%, so you set your trailing stop order at 10%. As the stock price climbs to $60, your trailing stop order follows it up, remainig 10% lower than the stock price ($54). Then if the stock drops back below $54, you trailing stop order will fill.
