| riˈzistəns |

DEFINITION: an imaginary line on a stock chart that represents a price that the stock can't seem to climb above. The more times a stock price has climbed towards the resistance and then fallen back down, the stronger the resistance becomes.

Resistance is the opposite of support.

As a part of technical analysis, resistance and support levels are used by many traders to formulate trading strategies. For example, if a stock is approaching a very strong resistance level, a trader may become bearish on the stock. Thus, the trader would close any bullish positions on the stock, or open bearish positions.

Once a stock is able to break through the resistance level and climb above it, the resistance level often becomes a support level.

A stock that continually bounces between support and resistance is often called a "channeling" or "wave" stock.
