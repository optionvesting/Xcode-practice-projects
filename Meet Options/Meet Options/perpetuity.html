|ˌpərpiˈt(y)o͞oitē|

DEFINITION: when you withdraw an amount of money equal to the rate of return on your money, so that the balance never decreases. This constant stream of identical cash flows will have no end as long as the rate of return maintains constant.

A perpetuity is the goal of many investors.

For example, if you put $1 million in your account and you achieve a 5% annual rate of return, your account will grow by $50,000 that first year. If at the end of that year you withdraw $50,000, your balance at the end of the year will be $1 million still, and you were able to withdraw $50,000! Next year your account gains another 5% and you withdraw another $50,000. You will be able to withdraw $50,000 every year while your account balance remains at $1 million infinitely.
