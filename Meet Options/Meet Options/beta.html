|ˈbātə|

DEFINITION: a measure of the volatility of a stock in relation to the broader market, also known as "beta coefficient".

Beta is calculated using regression analysis. The baseline value is 1, which indicates that the stock's price will move the same amount as the market.

A beta of less than 1 means that the stock is less volatile than the market.

A beta of greater than 1 indicates that the stock's price is more volatile than the market. A stock with a beta of 1.5 moves 50% more than the rest of the market. If the market goes up 1%, that stock should go up about 1.5%. If the market goes down 1%, that stock should go down about 1.5%.

The time value of options on a stock with a low beta is generally less than that of options on a stock with a high beta.
