|enwaɪesˈiː|

DEFINITION: the New York Stock Exchange is based in New York City, and is considered the largest equities-based exchange in the world, based on total market capitalization of its listed securities.

Formerly run as a private organization, the NYSE became a public entity in 2005. Later, in 2007 the NYSE merged with the European exchange, becoming known as NYSE Euronext.

Floor trading still exists at the NYSE, although more than half of all trades are executed electronically.

The origins of the exchange date all the way back to 1792. The NYSE is home to the majority of the world’s largest and best-known companies.

Many foreign based companies also trade their stock at the NYSE.

The NYSE opens for trading Monday through Friday 9:30a.m. to 4:00p.m. (ET), closing early on rare occasions.
