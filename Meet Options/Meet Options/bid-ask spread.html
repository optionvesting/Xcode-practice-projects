|ask| |bid| |spred|

DEFINITION: the difference between the ask price and the bid price. The ask price is always higher than the bid price.

For example, if the bid price is $50.50 and the ask price is $50.75 then the "bid-ask spread" is $0.25.

Options generally have a much larger bid-ask spread than stocks.

It is generally a good idea to use limit orders when there is a large bid-ask spread.
