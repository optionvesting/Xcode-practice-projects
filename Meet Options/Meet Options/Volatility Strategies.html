Open a long straddle.

PROs: Ultimate leverage; small initial investment; highest maximum profit
CONs: Low probability of profit; time decay works against you
➫ Probability of profit: ~25%
➫ Maximum profit: Unlimited
➫ Maximum loss: Entire initial investment (cost of buying the naked call and put)

When important news is expected on a stock that will likely cause the price of the stock to either drop or rise significantly, you can play that volatility with a long straddle.

Buy both a call and a put for the same stock. When you confirm the direction of the stock's movement after the important news, sell the losing option right away for a small loss and ride the winner for a large profit.

If the stock doesn't move significantly in either direction, then immediately sell both options for a small loss.
