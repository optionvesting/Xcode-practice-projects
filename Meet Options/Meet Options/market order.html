DEFINITION: a type of order where the market maker sets the price, not the investor. The market maker is required to fill all market orders immediately.

The market maker is constantly advertising his current prices. The ask is the price you will pay if you buy a stock or option with a market order. The bid is the price you will get if you sell a stock or option with a market order.

To obtain the current bid and ask prices, get a stock quote from one of many available websites.

When there is a large bid-ask spread, it is generally better to use a limit order instead of a market order. When there is a small bid-ask spread or you need the order filled immediately, use a market order.
