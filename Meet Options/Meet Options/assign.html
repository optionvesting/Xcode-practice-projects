|əˈsīn|

DEFINITION: when the person that sold an option is forced to sell (for a short call) or buy (for a short put) the underlying stock; the result of an option being exercised by the person that bought the option.

Most contracts will typically be exercised only if they expire in-the-money. However, any option can be exercised at any time prior to expiration.

If you bought the option, you own the long position of the option, and you can decide when/if to exercise the option. If you exercise the option, the market maker gets assigned the option and must adhere to the contract.

If you sell an option to open, you own the short position of the option, and you don't control when/if the option is exercised. You could get assigned the option at anytime, although it is unlikely to happen prior to expiration.
