Selecting your broker.

Remember that you are the client and your broker works for you! Carefully weigh the benefits provided by each available brokerage and choose what best meets your needs using this guide.

A brokerage is truly a personal preference, but there are several objective criteria that you can evaluate in order to make an educated choice.

The list below is sorted from most to least important to the average investor.

 

How much do trades cost?

All brokers charge a commission on every trade placed; that is the way they make money. Commissions typically vary between stocks and options, with options trades being slightly more expensive.

➫ Stock trades usually have a flat-fee commission, regardless of how many shares of stock are bought or sold.
➫ On the contrary, option trade commissions are usually based on a flat-fee plus a small per-contract fee.
➫ Commissions are also likely higher for orders placed over the phone with a live broker.
➫ If your brokerage provides the ability to trade via a web browser or mobile app, the commissions are likely the same for both of those mediums.

In recent years, several discount brokerage websites have been created. These websites include:
➫ optionsXpress
➫ optionshouse
➫ tradeMONSTER
➫ TradeKing

Commissions and fees with these discount brokerage websites are about half as much as you would pay with older, more established brokerages like TDAmeritrade, Scotttrade, and E-Trade.

 

How can I place trades?

Unless you are extremely old-fashioned and completely unable to use a computer (not likely since you are using this material!), you should select a brokerage with a strong online presence. That is because it always cheaper, faster, and downright easier to place trades online. Most brokerages now offer online trading, which is likely to be your primary access method to your account.

There are also other mediums through which you can place trades with some brokerages.

The ability to place orders and access your account over the phone can be an important feature if you expect to be in places or situations that won't allow you to have access to the internet. Although that situation is becoming less and less likely everyday, it still exists at the moment, so be sure to check and see if your brokerage offers that capability if you’re interested.

The biggest trend in placing trades is mobile trading (i.e. native and web apps accessible via your smartphone or tablet). Each broker has a different style of mobile capability. Most of their apps can be downloaded from an app store and used in demo mode, even before setting up an account with the brokerage, just to see if you like the interface.

Know that all brokers aren't created equally when it comes to placing trades. Some provide more intuitive and streamlined trading platforms to make the process as simple as possible, while others are still rather rudimentary with the online trading platforms.

Before selecting a new broker, or if you're planning to switch to a new broker, be sure to set up demo accounts with different brokerages to test out their trading platforms for free.

 

What promotions are available?

Like any other business, brokers are competing to win you over. It is common to find outstanding promotions offered by many brokerages. These promotions often include:
➫ free cash (up to $1,000!) deposited into your account
➫ commission-free trading for a specified period of time
➫ free products like electronics (i.e. tablets, smartphones)

Some of these promotions are conditional upon you depositing a certain amount of money into your account upon opening; others are not.

It is also common to find brokerages offering referral incentives, so if you have a friend refer you, it could be a win for all parties involved.

 

Can I paper trade?

Just like a crossover dribble, throwing a knuckleball, or anything else, your trading will get better with practice. The tutorials in this app provide invaluable information gained from experience of other traders, and you will learn even more as you place trades yourself.

Paper trading provides the perfect platform for trying new strategies and honing your skills. Many brokerages will give you an account of free “funny money” on the order of $50,000 or $100,000 that you can use however you choose. That way, the first time you do a complex credit spread or a risky play on volatility using naked calls and puts, it’s not with real money.

Paper trading allows you to learn important lessons, with the only cost being the time you put into the trade. Even if you’re ready to jump right in and start trading with real money, paper trading allows you go get accustomed to your new broker’s trading user-interface which is useful.

The importance of paper trading can't be overstated. Although it might not be necessary to practice low risk strategies like selling covered calls, it is highly recommended to try more complex and risky strategies at least once with a paper trading account.

If you decide to choose a brokerage that doesn’t offer paper trading, you can likely find another place online to paper trade, but you won’t get to experience how to place trades with your particular broker.

 

How easy is it to get options approval?

As discussed in an earlier module, there are different levels of option trading (usually five, but broker dependent), all of which require approval from your brokerage.

Factors that can affect your approval include cash available in your account and your own knowledge of option trading.

The information available in these modules is sufficient for any level of option trading, as long as you are able to thoroughly understand the material, and take advantage of paper trading to practice using different strategies.

Cash available is likely to be your biggest limiting factor. Regardless of how much money you have in your account, it doesn't hurt to request higher levels of option trading approval. Some brokers include credit checks in the approval process, while others factor in how long your account has been open.

If you are worried that you might not get approved for the level you want, try one of the discount brokerage websites mentioned above for you best chance at approval.

Remember, that even if you don’t get the highest level of approval, there are still loads of strategies available to you, and endless trading opportunities.

 

What research resources are provided?

This is likely one of the least important criterion in your decision of which brokerage to use. Not because research isn't important (it's incredibly important in fact!), but because there are so many freely accessible websites that provide a wealth of research resources.

However, if your trading style leads you to make many short-term trades on a daily basis, heat-index maps and other real-time streaming information can be useful, and is usually only available from your brokerage website.

Information common to online brokerage websites that can also be found on free sites like Google and Yahoo! finance include: stock charts, stock ratings, financial numbers like P/E, and economic calendar data.

 

What customer support is available?

When you have a question about your account, how to deposit or withdraw money, how to open or close positions, or whatever else, it can be very valuable and comforting to be able to talk to a human being and ask your question. Each brokerage offers a different amount of customer and technical support, available either by phone, email, or live online via chat. Most likely a discount brokerage will offer less live customer support than a more expensive brokerage.
