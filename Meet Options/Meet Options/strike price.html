|strīk| |prīs|

DEFINITION: the price at which an option contract can be used to buy (call option) or sell (put option) the underlying stock.

Call options have intrinsic value if the strike price is below the stock price. Put options have intrinsic value if the strike price is above the stock price.

For stocks priced between $5 and $25, call and put options are issued in $2.50 increments.

For stocks priced between $25 and $200, call and put options are issued in $5.00 increments.

For stocks priced above $200, call and put options are issued in $10.00 increments.
