|läNG| |pəˈziSHən|

DEFINITION: buying a stock or option to sell it later at a higher price for profit. Most casual investors only know how to open and close long positions, and don't know how to use short positions. They hope to buy a stock low and then sell high.

If you buy a stock and plan to sell it later for a profit, you are "long" on that stock. If you own McDonald's stock you are "long McDonald's."

A long position on stock is the most basic strategy used by bullish investors.

A long position is the opposite of a short position. A short position means selling a stock or option with the expectation it will decrease in value so you can buy it back later.

With a short position, you sell high and then buy low.
