|ˈsektər|

DEFINITION: a group or division of businesses that peform similar processes and/or provide similar services. Prominent sectors include: technology, aerospace, energy, construction, finance, retail, and pharmaceuticals.

Dividing stocks into sectors allows for in-depth analysis of the economy as a whole. Each sector has unique characteristics and a different risk profile.

Investors often compare one stock to other stocks in the same sector when performing financial analysis.
