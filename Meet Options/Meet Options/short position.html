|SHôrt| |pəˈziSHən|

DEFINITION: selling a stock or option to buy it back later at a lower price for profit. Most casual investors only know how to open and close long positions, and don't know how to use short positions. They hope to buy a stock low and then sell high later. Short positions allow traders to benefit from an expected decrease in price of a stock or option.

If you sell a stock and plan to buy it back later at a lower price, you are "short" that stock.

A short position on stock is the most basic strategy used by bearish investors.

With a short position, you sell high first, and then buy low.
