DEFINITION: a portfolio (group) of stocks and bonds that is professionally managed. The money used to purchase the stocks and bonds comes from a pool of money from many Individual investors.

By purchasing a single mutual fund, an individual investor invests in many different diversified assets. Many individual investors purchase mutual funds so they don't have to manage their own investments, they just let the professional manage the fund.

One main advantage of mutual funds is that since they are diversified, they are less risky than individual stocks.

One disadvantage of mutual funds is that the professionals that manage the fund charge fees. Also, mutual funds don't guarantee profits so individual investors can still lose money.
