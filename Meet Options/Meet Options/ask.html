|ask|

DEFINITION: the price at which the market maker will sell you a security (i.e. the price the market maker is "ask"-ing for the security).

Sometimes the ask price is simply referred to as "the ask." The ask price is always higher than the bid price.

If you place a market order to buy a security, you will pay the full ask price.

If you use a limit order to buy a security, you can usually buy the security for slightly less than the ask.

The ask is the opposite of bid, which is the price the market maker is willing to pay for a security.
