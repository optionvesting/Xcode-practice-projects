DEFINITION: a table of all option prices for a given stock. Option chains are separated by expiration date, and organized by strike price.

An option chain can be viewed in two basic forms: stacked and straddle. The stacked view shows all the call options first, with the put options listed below. A straddle view shows call options on the left and put options on the right.

Each option has a bid and ask price that is set by the market maker.

Different brokerages and websites show option chains in slightly different formats, but provide similar information.
