DEFINITION: an indicator that shows the average value of a stock's price over a set period of time. Moving averages are useful to measure momentum.

The chart of Under Armour (UA) below shows a 20 day simple moving average (SMA) in red and a 50 SMA in green.

A decreasing moving average indicates the stock is bearish. An increasing moving average indicates the stock is bullish.

When the shorter term moving average crosses the longer term moving average, that indicates momentum. An example of bullish momentum can be seen in Feb '13 on the chart above. At that time the red crossed above the green line, indicating bullish momentum. Since then, the stock has continued to increase 20%.
