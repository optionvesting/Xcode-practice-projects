|stəˈkastik| |ˈäsəˌlātər|

DEFINITION: a technical indicator that compares a security's closing price to its price range over a given time period. Investors often use the stochastic to determine when a stock is overbought (and possibly ready for a dip in price) or oversold (and possibly ready to bounce back up).

The chart below (retrieved from Google Finance) shows Apple stock over a 6 month period. Below the chart you see the slow stochastic (SSTO) which contains a blue and red line. When those lines are above 80, the stock may be overbought and ready for a dip. When the SSTO is below 20, the stock may be oversold and ready to climb a little.
