|ˈdeltə|

DEFINITION: a value that defines how much the price of an option changes in relation to the price of the underlying stock.

For example, if an option has a delta of .5, when the price of the underlying stock goes $1.00, the price of that option will go up about $0.50.

Options with more time value than instrinsic value will have a lower delta, and are less volatile. Options with more intrinsic value have a higher delta, and their price will move more when the underlying stock price moves.

The delta of an option changes constantly, as time and intrinsic values change.

The delta of a call option is represented as a positive number, because the price of a call varies directly with the price of the underlying stock.

The delta of a put option is represented as a negative number, because the price of a put varies indirectly (opposite) with the price of the underlying stock.
