DEFINITION: opening a short position on a security. That means selling something that you don't already own, with the plan to buy it back later at a lower price. To profit from a short position, you first sell high and then buy low.

Selling short is a basic bearish strategy for stock trading. First, place a "sell-to-open" order. The money you make from selling the stock will go into your investment account, but the broker will place a hold on the money because you are required to buy the stock back later. After the stock has dropped in price, place a "buy-to-close" order.

Selling short is the opposite of a long position. If you short sell McDonald's stock, you are "short McDonald's."

Short positions are the key to consistent gains and profits with options trading, because they take advantage of time value decay.
