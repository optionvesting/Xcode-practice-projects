|ˈkän trakt|

DEFINITION: an agreement that authorizes the purchase or sale of a given stock at a specific price. One option contract always controls 100 shares of stock.

The stock that can be bought or sold with an option contract is called the "underlying stock."

The specific price at which the underlying stock can be bought or sold is called the "strike price."

A call option contract is used to purchase 100 shares of stock at the strike price. A put option contract is used to sell 100 shares of the underlying stock.
