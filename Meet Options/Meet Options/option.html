DEFINITION: a contract that controls the purchase or sale of 100 shares of a given stock. The contract offers the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy (call) or sell (put) 100 shares of the underlying stock, anytime prior to the option's expiration date.

The price at which the option lets you buy or sell the stock is called the "strike price." The stock controlled by the option is called the "underlying stock."

There are two basic types of options: calls and puts. A call option lets the owner buy 100 shares of the underlying stock, and a put option lets the owner sell 100 shares of the underlying stock.

When the price of the underlying stock goes up, the price of the call options goes up and the price of the put options goes down.

When the price of the underlying stock goes down, the price of the call options goes down and the price of the put options goes up.
