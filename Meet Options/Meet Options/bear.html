DEFINITION: an investor who believes that a particular stock or market is going to drop in price.

To remember that a bear is expecting prices to drop, visualize a bear swiping down with his paw on prey.

Bears attempt to profit from a decline in prices. Bears are generally pessimistic about the state of a given market.

For example, if an investor is bearish on Google stock, they will use a strategy to attempt to profit from the stock's decline.

A bear is the opposite of a bull, who believes that a stock, sector, or market is going to rise in price.

Using stocks, the only way a bear can profit is with short positions. Using options, there are many strategies, both long and short, that a bear can use to profit.
