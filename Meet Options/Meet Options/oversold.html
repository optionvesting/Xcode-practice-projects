| ōvərˈsōld |

DEFINITION: a situation caused by low demand for a stock, in which the price of the stock has dropped to an artificially low level.

A technical indicator called "stochastic oscillator" or just "stochastic" for short helps investors identify oversold stocks. Oversold conditions can also be determined through the relative strength index (RSI).

When a stock is oversold, it is generally interpreted as a sign that the price of the asset is becoming undervalued and may experience an upward bounce.

An oversold security is the opposite of one that is overbought.
