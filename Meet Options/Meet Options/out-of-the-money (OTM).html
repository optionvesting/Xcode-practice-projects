|out| |əv| |T͟Hē| |ˈmənē|

DEFINTION: an option that has no intrinsic value. A call is out-of-the-money when its strike price is higher than the price of the underlying stock. A put is out-of-the-money when its strike price is lower than the price of the underlying stock.

If an option is out-of-the-money when it expires, it becomes worthless because there is no more time left for it to become in-the-money. Expired out-of-the-money options are removed from your account automatically.
