|in| |T͟Hē| |ˈmənē|

DEFINITION: an option that currently has intrinsic value (i.e has current worth). Options without intrinsic value are called "out-of-the-money."

Call options are "in-the-money" when the strike price is BELOW the current market price of the underlying stock. In-the-money calls let you buy stock for less than the market price.

Put options are "in-the-money" when the strike price is ABOVE the current market price of the underlying stock. In-the-money puts let you sell stock for more than the market price.
