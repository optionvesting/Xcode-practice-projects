| səˈpôrt |

DEFINITION: an imaginary line on a stock chart that represents a price that the stock can't seem to fall below. The more times a stock price has fallen towards the support and then bounces back up, the stronger the support becomes.

Resistance is the opposite of support.

As a part of technical analysis, resistance and support levels are used by many traders to formulate trading strategies. For example, if a stock is approaching a very strong support level, a trader may become bullish on the stock. Thus, the trader would close any bearish positions on the stock, or open bullish positions.

Once a stock falls through the support level, the old support level often becomes a new resistance level.

A stock that continually bounces between support and resistance is often called a "channeling" or "wave" stock.
