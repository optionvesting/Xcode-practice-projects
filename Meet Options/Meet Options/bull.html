DEFINITION: an investor who believes that the value of a particular stock or market is going to increase.

To remember that a bull is expecting prices to increase, visualize a bull slashing upward with its horns.

Bulls attempt to profit from an increase in prices. Bulls are generally optimistic about a particular stock or the market as a whole.

For example, if an investor is bullish on Google stock, they will use a strategy to attempt to profit from the stock's rise in price.

A bull is the opposite of a bear, who believes that a stock, sector, or market is going to drop in price.

Using stocks, the only way for a bull to profit is with a long position. Using options, there are many strategies, both long and short, that a bull can use to profit.
